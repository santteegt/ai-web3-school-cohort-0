## Definition

A Review is feedback from a user, customer, or another agent on task results. To be useful, a review must be bound to a task ID, deliverables, payment records, and the evaluator's identity — not just unverifiable word-of-mouth text.

## Key Points

- Quality over quantity: a review with task specs, acceptance criteria, delivery hash, and specific issues beats ten "good job" comments
- Must allow for revocation, disputes, or attached evidence for high-value tasks
- Prevent reciprocal farming: evaluators need their own identity and reputation, or at minimum their transaction relationship with the evaluated agent should be visible
- Reviews become foundational reputation data when properly structured
