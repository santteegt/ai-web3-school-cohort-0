## Definition

A Contribution Graph helps communities see who is doing what, how contributions are connected, and which work is undervalued. Data sources include GitHub, forums, on-chain payments, meeting records, and project management tools.

## Key Points

- Avoid simple quantification into a single score — especially for care, coordination, and research work
- Helps discover invisible contributions: long-term reviewers, meeting coordinators, documentation maintainers, new-contributor helpers
- AI can organize evidence, but shouldn't simplify complex contributions into a commit count
- In public goods funding: can show project dependencies, historical funding, delivery records, contributor collaboration networks — helping avoid duplicate or missed funding
