## Definition

A Proposal Summary organizes long governance proposals into structured sections: goals, background, budget, execution plan, risks, and pending questions. A good summary preserves key sources, objections, unanswered questions, and scope of impact — it does not just shorten text into a promotional piece.

## Key Points

- Fixed sections for horizontal comparison: goals, background, budget, executor, timeline, impact scope, support reasons, opposition reasons, open questions, relevant history
- Must mark uncertainty: "whether a budget is reasonable" is a judgment (not fact); "proposal requests 50,000 USDC" is a fact — these must not be mixed
- AI summaries should never be the basis for voting without source links to original materials
- Controversial proposals must not be reduced to a single supportive framing
