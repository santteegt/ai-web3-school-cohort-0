## Definition

Plurality emphasizes preserving differences and coordinating cooperation within a diverse group, rather than compressing everyone into an average opinion. Governance AI should help present reasons, preferences, and concerns of different groups to support negotiation rather than just outputting the loudest or most common viewpoints.

## Key Points

- AI summaries should not just give "mainstream opinion" but show minority concerns, stakeholder differences, regional/role differences, and room for negotiation
- Example: for a protocol fee proposal, LP, trader, developer, treasury, and long-term token holder perspectives all differ — governance AI should expand these perspectives, not smooth over conflicts
- Practical product meaning: expand perspectives rather than average them
- Related to the Plurality Institute's ideas about public collaboration in diversity
