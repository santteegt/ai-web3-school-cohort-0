## Definition

A Data Market discusses how data is authorized, priced, verified, and reused for AI systems. Data markets are difficult to build because data is not a typical commodity: it can be copied, quality is hard to judge in advance, authorization is complex, and privacy/copyright risks are high.

## Key Points

- More realistic as: datasets with verifiable sources, training/fine-tuning data with authorization terms, reproducible data processing pipelines, auditable contribution records, task-specific labeling/evaluation samples
- On-chain records are better for: authorizations, hashes, versions, contributions, and revenue distribution — NOT for storing large volumes of raw data
- Quality judgment challenges: buyers can't fully evaluate before purchase; reputation systems and trusted evaluators needed
- Privacy and copyright remain unsolved challenges at scale
