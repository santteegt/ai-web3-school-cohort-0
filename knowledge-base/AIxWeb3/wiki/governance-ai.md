## Definition

Governance AI helps governance participants better read proposals, track meetings, understand budgets, preserve sources, discover collaboration relationships, and reduce information asymmetry in critical decisions — without letting AI vote for the community or replace human political judgment.

## Key Points

- AI can summarize but must preserve sources and uncertainties in all governance outputs
- Separation required: facts vs. inferences vs. opinions vs. suggestions must not be lumped together
- Humans retain decision power: voting and authorization must be completed by humans or explicit rules
- AI summaries must not quietly vote for users or hide objections
- AI-assisted governance tools must be transparent, questionable, and reviewable

## Position in AI × Web3

Governance AI connects AI summarization with Web3 public decision-making. It can read on-chain voting, forum discussions, budget flows, and contribution records. Web3 provides public records, verifiable contributions, and transparent budgets — not just a "livelier community tool."
