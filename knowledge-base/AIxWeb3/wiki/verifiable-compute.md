## Definition

Verifiable Compute focuses on making off-chain calculation results verifiable by on-chain or third parties. AI inference is one type of calculation — broader scenarios include risk scoring, data aggregation, task evaluation, proof generation, and batch settlement.

## Key Points

- Off-chain execution + on-chain verification: expensive calculations stay off-chain; summaries, proofs, or signed results go on-chain
- Retains on-chain verifiability without stuffing complex inference into contracts
- Data availability must be considered: when only a hash or proof is on-chain, users need to know where original input/output is stored, who can access it, and how long it's kept
- Foundation for practical AI × Web3 systems that bridge expensive computation with on-chain trust
