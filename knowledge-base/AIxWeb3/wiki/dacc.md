## Definition

d/acc stands for defensive accelerationism — emphasizing the acceleration of defensive, decentralized, and human-enhancement technologies rather than just stronger automation. Proposed by Vitalik Buterin in "My techno-optimism" as a techno-optimistic framework that values defense, democracy, decentralization, and differential development.

## Key Points

- AI × Web3 practical meaning: build "better defensive tools" alongside "stronger automated agents"
- Examples: safer wallets, anti-phishing interfaces, verifiable agent logs, privacy-preserving data collaboration, open-source model evaluations, decentralized identity, reputation systems
- "Defensive" does not mean anti-technology — it means technology that protects individuals and their sovereignty
- Connected to CROPS: both emphasize that crypto/AI advancement should not sacrifice user control, privacy, and security
- Extended further in "d/acc: one year later" linking with crypto values: decentralization, censorship resistance, open global collaboration, stronger defensive technologies
