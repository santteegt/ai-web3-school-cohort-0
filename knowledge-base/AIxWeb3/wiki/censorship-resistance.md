## Definition

Censorship Resistance focuses on whether AI services, models, data, and agent identities can be blocked or deleted by a single point of failure. When AI systems become information gateways, censorship risk is not just "a webpage can't open" — it can manifest as models refusing legal tasks, platforms taking down agents, APIs banning regions, or training data filtering viewpoints.

## Key Points

- Censorship-resistant design includes: open-source clients, multi-model fallbacks, self-hostable endpoints, migratable identities, decentralized storage, on-chain or signed audit records
- Not every product needs complete decentralization, but critical public infrastructure should avoid single-point control
- For public goods, governance, cross-border collaboration, and open developer ecosystems: censorship resistance is about service availability, data retention, and identity continuity
