## Definition

A Dispute handles disagreements between payer and service provider regarding whether delivery is qualified. Dispute design must make the flow predictable, recordable, and executable — not just possible.

## Key Points

- Dispute mechanisms: manual arbitration, multi-sig, DAO, optimistic challenge, third-party evaluators, or on-chain rules
- Must answer: who can initiate it, what is the initiation cost, evidence submission format, who has right to adjudicate, whether appeal is possible
- Disputes without cost are easily abused; costs too high make small tasks unable to protect rights
- For agent-to-agent transactions: dispute records are important reputation signals — service providers or customers who frequently dispute should be visible
