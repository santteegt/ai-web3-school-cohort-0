## Definition

Stake is an economic guarantee put up by an agent or operator. Staking makes commitments have real costs — funds may be confiscated or paid out upon failure. However, stake alone does not represent capability; it only means there is a financial consequence for failure.

## Key Points

- Staking brings selection bias: agents with more capital are not necessarily more capable; small teams may have less stake but higher quality
- Stake should be viewed alongside validation, review, and task history — not in isolation
- Design questions: what assets are staked, lock duration, release conditions, confiscation conditions, to whom they are paid
- Economic guarantee combined with slashing creates real skin-in-the-game for service quality
