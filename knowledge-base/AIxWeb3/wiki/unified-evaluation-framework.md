## Definition

A 7-question framework applied consistently across every AI × Web3 direction to determine whether a project genuinely requires both AI and Web3, has clear stakeholder roles, defined automation boundaries, and an honest failure mode analysis.

## The 7 Questions

1. **Would this problem still exist without AI?** What capability does AI actually provide?
2. **Would this problem still exist without Web3?** What mechanism does Web3 actually provide?
3. **Who initiates the task, who executes it, who pays, who accepts the result, who bears the cost of failure, and who handles governance or arbitration?**
4. **Which actions can be automated, and which actions require human confirmation?**
5. **How can the result be verified? Is the cost of verification lower than the cost of human coordination?**
6. **Is it closer to:** application-layer experience, developer tooling, protocol/standard, permission system, security mechanism, or governance coordination process?
7. **If this direction fails, is it most likely because:** demand does not exist, trust cannot be established, costs are too high, interfaces are immature, permission risk is too high, or users are unwilling to change their workflow?

## Usage Notes

- Questions 1+2 form the "mutual necessity test" — both AI and Web3 must be genuinely indispensable
- Question 3 maps out the full stakeholder chain, revealing who is left without accountability if the system fails
- Question 4 defines the human-in-the-loop boundary — critical for wallet/permission directions
- Question 7 prevents optimism bias by naming the most likely failure mode before starting
