## Definition

The Payment and Commerce direction covers how AI agents buy APIs, data, compute, and services — and how quoting, acceptance, escrow, dispute handling, and settlement form a closed loop. The real difficulty is not whether money can be transferred, but how the full commercial flow connects into a controllable, verifiable process.

## Key Points

- When an AI agent acts as consumer or service provider, budget, payment conditions, delivery standards, and failure remedies must be defined upfront
- Payment is only one segment — commerce also includes service discovery, price negotiation, task execution, result acceptance, dispute handling, and settlement
- On-chain records provide receipts and state, but cannot automatically solve service quality, arbitration, and trust issues
- A valid AI × Web3 payment direction requires budget control, proof of delivery, verifiable records, and dispute handling — not just clicking a payment button
