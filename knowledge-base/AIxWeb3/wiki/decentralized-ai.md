## Definition

Decentralized AI is a redesign of how data, models, compute, inference, evaluation, and revenue distribution are organized within AI systems — not "putting models on the chain." What AI systems truly need to decentralize is not necessarily the model itself, but the control over critical resources and key decisions.

## Key Points

- Layer first, then judge value: data, models, compute, inference, evaluation, settlement, governance should not be lumped into a single term
- Openness comes at an engineering cost: distributed networks introduce latency, quality fluctuations, Sybil attacks, and governance inefficiencies
- The chain is better suited as a coordination layer: recording ownership, incentivizing contributions, settling tasks, governing parameters
- Centralization provides convenience; the question is identifying stages where open networks provide better verifiability, composability, and resistance to single-point control
- Evaluate any decentralized AI project by: what it decentralizes, what the on-chain part does, what it improves vs. centralized, and whether quality/governance costs are worth it

## Position in AI × Web3

Decentralized AI is foundational infrastructure that influences agents, payments, data, models, privacy, verification, and governance — but doesn't necessarily turn directly into a user product.
