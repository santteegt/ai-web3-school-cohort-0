## Definition

The Governance, Coordination, and Public Goods direction covers how AI can help DAOs, communities, and public goods projects with proposal summaries, meeting action items, contribution records, budget checklists, and transparent execution — while keeping governance power, budget approvals, and final judgment in human hands.

## Key Points

- AI-suitable tasks: proposal summaries, organizing discussion threads, turning meetings into action items, contribution records, budget execution checklists, community Q&A
- Not suitable for AI delegation: value judgments, budget approvals, punishment/incentive decisions, initiating irreversible actions on behalf of the community
- Web3 provides: public records, verifiable contributions, transparent budgets, and open collaboration mechanisms — not just a "livelier community tool"
- An AI governance assistant that auto-generates and passes budget proposals without human confirmation and public discussion is a governance risk, not an efficiency improvement
