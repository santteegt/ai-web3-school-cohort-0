## Definition

A Quality Benchmark answers whether a model, node, or data source is actually reliable in an open network. Standard benchmarks only test fixed datasets; decentralized AI requires stability, reproducibility, gaming-resistance, and real-scenario alignment.

## Key Points

- Dimensions: result stability, whether nodes execute as agreed, latency/failure rate acceptability, output reproducibility or sampling verification, gaming/overfitting/collusion detection, real-product scenario alignment
- Should feed into routing, pricing, reputation, and settlement — not just serve leaderboards
- A node performing consistently well receives more traffic; a high-failure-rate model should not be routed to high-risk scenarios
- Gaming (metric manipulation) is a specific risk in decentralized settings with financial incentives
