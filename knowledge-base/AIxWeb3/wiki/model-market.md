## Definition

A Model Market is a marketplace where model capabilities can be discovered, compared, called, and paid for as services. A truly useful model market solves discovery, evaluation, routing, and settlement — not just listing models.

## Key Points

- Key issue for builders: replaceability — if a task can be completed by multiple models, the system needs model routing decisions (cost, privacy, capability, verifiability tradeoffs)
- Model selection dimensions: classification/extraction (cheap), complex reasoning (high-capability), privacy content (local), code/images/on-chain (specialized)
- Discovery alone is not enough — evaluation, quality benchmarks, and settlement are all required components
- Foundation for [[decentralized-ai]] compute economy
