## Definition

DIDs (Decentralized Identifiers) provide resolvable identities not bound to a specific chain. VCs (Verifiable Credentials) carry claims issued by a specific issuer — e.g., "this agent passed a capability test" or "this agent is operated by a certain team." Together they form a cross-platform identity and verification model.

## Key Points

- Advantage of DID/VC: universality — not limited to one chain, cross-platform compatible
- VC credibility depends on the issuer — anyone can issue a claim, but not all claims are trustworthy
- Products should display: issuer, issuance time, revocation status, and verification path
- VCs for agents can carry capability proofs, organizational affiliations, audit passes, and compliance declarations
