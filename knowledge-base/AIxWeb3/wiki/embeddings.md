## Definition

An embedding is a dense vector representation of text, code, or another object that maps it into a high-dimensional space so that semantically similar items are geometrically close. Embeddings are the foundation of semantic search, RAG retrieval, and clustering.

## Key Points

- Embeddings are **good at finding relevant material** — measuring semantic closeness between a query and documents
- Embeddings are **not suitable for deciding whether a conclusion is correct** — high cosine similarity does not mean the retrieved content is accurate or current
- The same concept expressed differently (e.g. "gas fee" vs "transaction cost") should have similar embedding vectors
- In RAG, embeddings power the vector database lookup — but metadata filters (version, chain, date) must augment pure similarity search
- Embeddings are generated by an encoder model and stored in a [[vector-database]]; retrieval is approximate, not exact
