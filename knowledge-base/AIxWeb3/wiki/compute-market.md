## Definition

A Compute Market is a marketplace where GPUs, CPUs, storage, bandwidth, and inference services become purchasable resources. Open compute networks can provide new supply sources when centralized cloud is expensive, queued, or restricted — but significant engineering challenges remain.

## Key Points

- Difficulties: unstable node performance (latency/throughput fluctuate), GPU/driver/image/network reproducibility, task result verification, remote nodes not inherently trustworthy for private data, SLA guarantees harder for open networks
- Better start: containerized inference, batch processing tasks, non-critical workloads, repeat-verifiable tasks
- High-value/private-data/real-time: require additional measures (TEE, ZK, redundant execution, sampling checks, human auditing)
- Cost efficiency advantage must be weighed against quality and reliability risks
