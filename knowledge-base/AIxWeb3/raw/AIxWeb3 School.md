AI x Web3 is not just two buzzwords placed together. A real system often involves all of these at the same time:

-   how models understand tasks, organize context, and call tools
-   how Agents move from "answering questions" into "execution workflows"
-   how wallets and accounts express permissions, spending limits, time windows, and revocation conditions
-   how on-chain transactions are simulated, confirmed, recorded, and audited
-   how payments, identity, reputation, and governance connect to automated systems
-   how privacy, security, and verifiability become part of the product

If you only look from the AI side, you will underestimate asset and permission risks. If you only look from the Web3 side, you will miss the complexity of models, context, and tool orchestration.

## [Handbook Outline](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/#handbook-outline)

This Handbook is organized into four groups: AI Foundations, Web3 Foundations, AI × Web3 Bridge, and Frontier Exploration.

You can think of it as a four-layer map:

### [AI Foundations](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/#ai-foundations)

Start by understanding the basic components of models and application systems:

-   [LLM](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/ai/llm/): what large models can do, and what they cannot replace.
-   [Prompt](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/ai/prompt/): how to write task goals, boundaries, and output formats clearly.
-   [Context](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/ai/context/): what a model can see in one run, which information is trustworthy, and what expires.
-   [RAG](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/ai/rag/): how to connect external knowledge, sources, and citations to a model.
-   [Agent](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/ai/agent/): how models enter tool use and multi-step execution.
-   [Frameworks](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/ai/frameworks/): understand which orchestration layer LangChain, LangGraph, Agents SDK, and similar frameworks solve.
-   [MCP](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/ai/mcp/): understand how models, tools, and context are connected through protocols.
-   [Evaluation](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/ai/evaluation/): make model outputs, Agent behavior, and tool calls testable, replayable, and continuously improvable.

### [Web3 Foundations](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/#web3-foundations)

Then add the basic intuition of on-chain systems:

-   [Network](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/web3/network/): understand the basic environment of blocks, consensus, L2, RPC, and on-chain state.
-   [Cryptography](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/web3/cryptography/): hashes, public/private keys, signatures, and other underlying concepts.
-   [Wallet](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/web3/wallet/): a wallet is not just a login button; it is the entry point for identity and signatures.
-   [Smart Contract](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/web3/smart-contract/): how on-chain rules are deployed, called, and used to update state.
-   [Account Abstraction](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/web3/account-abstraction/): why Smart Accounts are better suited for expressing Agent permissions.
-   [DeFi](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/web3/defi/): understand assets, liquidity, lending, and risk propagation in open financial protocols.
-   [Oracle](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/web3/oracle/): understand how off-chain data enters on-chain systems, and why data sources become risk points.
-   [Indexing](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/web3/indexing/): organize on-chain events, transactions, and state into a data layer usable by AI and products.
-   [Security](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/web3/security/): risk boundaries in contracts, permissions, simulation, and monitoring.

### [AI × Web3 Bridge](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/#ai-%C3%97-web3-bridge)

Then move into the real intersection problems:

-   [Chain-aware Context](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/chain-aware-context/): how on-chain state enters Agent context.
-   [Web3 Tool Use](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/web3-tool-use/): how RPC, wallet, and contract tools are called by Agents.
-   [Agent Workflow](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/agent-workflow/): which steps are suitable for automation, and which must stay human-in-the-loop.
-   [Agent Wallet](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/agent-wallet/): what permissions an Agent can receive, and how to limit and revoke them.
-   [Machine Payment](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/machine-payment/): how machines complete micro-payments and service settlement.
-   [Settlement & Escrow](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/settlement-and-escrow/): how automated transactions, service delivery, and dispute handling complete settlement.
-   [Agent Identity](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/agent-identity/): how Agents are identified, authorized, and tracked for responsibility boundaries.
-   [Agent Trust & Reputation](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/agent-trust-and-reputation/): how to record Agent behavior, capabilities, and trustworthy history.
-   [Verifiable AI](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/verifiable-ai/): how model outputs, execution processes, and results can be verified.
-   [AI Security](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/ai-security/): how Prompt Injection, tool abuse, permission isolation, and audit logs enter system design.
-   [AI Privacy](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/ai-privacy/): privacy boundaries between user data, on-chain identity, and model context.
-   [Governance AI](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/bridge/governance-ai/): how AI enters proposal summarization, governance collaboration, voting assistance, and public decision-making.

### [Frontier Exploration](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/#frontier-exploration)

Frontier Exploration combines the previous knowledge into prototypes that can be built, demoed, and extended:

-   [Agentic Commerce](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/tracks/agentic-commerce/): how Agents discover services, negotiate tasks, complete payments, and leave receipts.
-   [Wallet / Permission](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/tracks/wallet-permission/): product prototypes around wallets, permissions, Session Keys, Policies, and Guards.
-   [AI Security](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/tracks/ai-security/): turn attack surfaces, permission isolation, audits, and alerts into demonstrable systems.
-   [Governance](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/tracks/governance/): AI collaboration tools for DAOs, public goods, and protocol governance.
-   [Dev Tooling](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/tracks/dev-tooling/): tools for contract understanding, testing, documentation, code review, and developer workflows.
-   [Open Track](https://aiweb3.school/en/handbook/tracks/open-track/): reserved for new intersection problems and practice directions that have not yet become fixed categories.
