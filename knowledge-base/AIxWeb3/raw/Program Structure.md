## Program structure

-   **Week 1 | Bootcamp: AI and Web3 foundations**
    
    Build a shared language by connecting LLMs, prompts, workflows, agents, tool use, AI coding, wallets, signatures, transactions, gas, smart contracts, and testnets into one execution chain. Participants complete Vibe Coding / Hermes Agent practice, create a test wallet, make a testnet transaction, deploy or call a minimal smart contract, and document successes, failures, and human corrections.
    
-   **Week 2 | Bootcamp: AI × Web3 intersection areas**
    
    Enter the real problem space through tracks such as Agentic Commerce / Payment, Dev Tooling, AI Security / Privacy, AI × Governance / Coordination, and Open Track. Participants choose one direction, map out who initiates the task, who executes, who pays, who verifies, and who carries the risk, then produce a proposal ready for the next phase.
    
-   **Week 3 | Practice deepening and Hackathon kickoff**
    
    Participants continue filling gaps and complete smaller exercises such as agent workflow + wallet confirmation, on-chain receipts, testnet payments, governance proposal summaries, risk-control strategies, or verifiable execution records. In parallel, teams finalize the hackathon track, project topic, target users, technical path, roles, and demo plan.
    
-   **Week 4 | Hackathon sprint and Demo showcase**
    
    Teams focus on core implementation, basic testing, README, demo flow, and necessary on-chain or tool-call records, with feedback from mentor office hours. Final submissions include project name, track, target user, problem definition, demo link, repository, README, video, and relevant contract addresses, testnet addresses, transaction hashes, or other verification materials.
