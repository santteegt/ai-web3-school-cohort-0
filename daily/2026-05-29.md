### Weekly Review Sharing Draft (due May 31)

**Reflection on Week 2 so far:**

Week 2 opened with a deliberate shift in orientation: rather than consuming more concepts, the goal became deciding which AI × Web3 directions genuinely require both domains — and which are conceptual mashups. To do that, I skimmed all 15 chapters of the AIxWeb3 Bridge section, built a visual mental model of the 8 core bridge modules (execution spine + identity/trust foundation), and produced a full problem space map covering the 6 foundational directions (Payment/Commerce, Identity/Reputation, Capability/Interoperability, Wallet/Permission, Privacy/Security, Governance/Coordination), analyzing the AI role, Web3 mechanism, and the "remove one domain and the system breaks" test for each.

The most useful insight from this analysis was the Direction Evaluation Matrix: the five criteria — Structural Demand, Verifiability, Minimal Entry Point, Risk Boundaries, Follow-Through — form a filter, not a checklist. A direction that scores well on entry point but has no structural demand beyond a current hot project is a trap. That filter is what led me to choose **Identity / Reputation / Capability / Interoperability** as my main track: it has long-term structural demand (multi-agent systems need open discovery and verifiable reputation regardless of which specific projects survive), a clear minimal entry point (semantic capability matching above ERC-8004/A2A), and direct follow-through into Week 3 and the Hackathon.

My secondary direction is **Governance / Coordination**: AI-assisted synthesis of governance proposals + on-chain binding execution is a strong demonstration case with clear AI and Web3 roles, and the risk boundaries (AI must not initiate governance actions) almost write the design spec for you.

Next steps for the remainder of Week 2: complete the problem breakdown (stakeholders, automation boundaries, human gates, verification), draft the initial project proposal, build the reference list, and write the direction backlog (capturing why the other 4 directions were de-prioritized so I don't revisit them without new information).
