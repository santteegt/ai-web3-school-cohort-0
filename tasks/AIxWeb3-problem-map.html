AI × Web3 — Problem Space Map

Six foundational directions where AI capabilities and Web3 mechanisms are both indispensable. Neither domain alone solves the problem.

💳 Payment / Commerce 🪪 Identity / Reputation 🔌 Capability / Interop 🔐 Wallet / Permission 🛡 Privacy / Security 🏛 Governance / Coordination

Direction Map — Hexagonal View

Direction Breakdown — AI Role · Web3 Mechanism · Why Both Are Required

💳
Direction 1
Payment / Commerce / Settlement
🤖
AI Role
Understands the economic flow. Parses service intent, discovers providers, evaluates quotes, verifies delivery quality, detects anomalies, and reasons about disputes and failure modes. AI turns natural language intent into a structured payment lifecycle.
⛓
Web3 Mechanism
Enforces and settles the flow. Programmable money (x402, MPP), smart-contract escrow (ERC-8183), L2 micropayments, on-chain receipts, and trustless multi-party settlement. State machine: pending → locked → delivered → accepted → released.
Why both are required
–AI
Web3 can settle funds on-chain but cannot understand service descriptions, evaluate whether a delivery actually meets the spec, or reason about disputes. A dumb escrow with no delivery oracle is just locked money.
–Web3
AI can parse intent and judge quality, but cannot trustlessly move funds, enforce spending limits, or generate tamper-proof payment receipts. Without Web3, "I'll pay after delivery" remains a trust assumption.
✦ Main Track
🪪
Direction 2
Identity / Reputation
🤖
AI Role
Understands what agents can do and how well. Parses capability manifests, matches services to user goals via semantic search, evaluates task quality for reputation scoring, and detects behavioral anomalies across delivery records.
⛓
Web3 Mechanism
Makes identity and reputation portable and tamper-proof. DIDs/Verifiable Credentials, ERC-8004 on-chain agent registry, EAS attestations, immutable task delivery records, and stake/slashing for reputation enforcement.
Why both are required
–AI
Web3 can store attestations on-chain, but cannot evaluate the semantic meaning of capability claims, judge task quality, or match an agent's profile to a user's intent. A registry without understanding is just a phone book.
–Web3
AI can infer reputation from behavior patterns, but those inferences aren't portable or verifiable across systems. Without Web3, reputation lives in one platform's database and disappears when the platform changes.
🔌
Direction 3
Capability / Interoperability
🤖
AI Role
Orchestrates across agents and tools. Translates between capability formats (MCP, A2A), composes multi-agent workflows, maps natural language goals to structured tool calls, and resolves which combination of capabilities satisfies a user intent.
⛓
Web3 Mechanism
Provides open, trustless discovery and invocation. On-chain capability registries (ERC-8004), standardized agent-to-agent protocols (A2A), payment for capability access (MPP), and open provenance records for auditable cross-agent invocations.
Why both are required
–AI
Web3 can register capabilities in an open registry, but cannot plan which capabilities to combine for a given goal, resolve ambiguity, or handle errors gracefully across service calls. Composition requires reasoning, not just lookup.
–Web3
AI can orchestrate agents, but without open registries and payment primitives there's no trustless discovery or economic incentive for third-party agents to expose capabilities. Multi-agent systems collapse into closed ecosystems.
🔐
Direction 4
Wallet / Permission / Safe Execution
🤖
AI Role
Classifies risk and translates intent to permission specs. Interprets transaction simulation results, detects spending anomalies, converts natural language task scope into structured authorization boundaries, and identifies when a write action crosses a risk threshold requiring human confirmation.
⛓
Web3 Mechanism
Enforces permissions at the cryptographic layer. ERC-4337 smart accounts, session keys, EIP-7702 delegation, on-chain policy enforcement, revocable Pacts (Cobo model), and immutable execution logs that can't be deleted post-hoc.
Why both are required
–AI
Web3 can enforce permissions at the protocol level, but cannot dynamically assess the semantic risk of an action, understand why an agent is requesting a permission, or flag unusual behavior patterns before they result in an irreversible transaction.
–Web3
AI can model risk and generate permission specs, but cannot cryptographically enforce them or prevent unauthorized signing. Without smart accounts and session keys, permissions exist only as policy text — enforceable only by trust.
🛡
Direction 5
Privacy / Security / Sovereignty
🤖
AI Role
Detects semantic and behavioral threats. Threat modeling, prompt injection detection, audit log analysis, anomaly detection in tool calls, attack surface classification, and identifying when agent behavior has been manipulated by adversarial inputs.
⛓
Web3 Mechanism
Provides cryptographic proof and sovereign infrastructure. Zero-knowledge proofs for privacy-preserving computation, TEEs for confidential execution, on-chain audit trails that can't be deleted, censorship resistance, and user key ownership enabling portability without vendor lock-in.
Why both are required
–AI
Web3 provides ZKPs and immutable logs, but cannot detect prompt injection, social engineering, or model-layer manipulation. An on-chain audit trail records what happened — it doesn't prevent the agent from being tricked into signing a malicious transaction.
–Web3
AI can detect threats but cannot provide cryptographic proof of correct computation or sovereign data storage. Without Web3, "trust the AI" remains a vendor trust assumption — users cannot independently verify what the model actually did or export their data.
◈ Secondary Track
🏛
Direction 6
Governance / Coordination / Public Goods
🤖
AI Role
Reduces information overload and surfaces actionable structure. Summarizes proposals, extracts meeting action items, models budget impact, synthesizes community sentiment, and tracks contribution records — but cannot make value judgments, approve budgets, or replace community deliberation.
⛓
Web3 Mechanism
Makes governance binding, transparent, and auditable. On-chain voting (Snapshot, Governor), transparent treasury execution, verifiable contribution records, DAO coordination primitives, and immutable decision logs that prevent retroactive revision of who voted for what.
Why both are required
–AI
Web3 can record votes and execute treasury transfers on-chain, but cannot synthesize complex proposals into accessible summaries, reduce participation fatigue, or help communities understand the downstream implications of a governance decision.
–Web3
AI can summarize proposals and suggest actions, but without Web3 there is no way to make those decisions binding, tamper-proof, or trustlessly executable. AI governance without on-chain enforcement is just a well-organized Notion doc.
